About me.

I am a Ph.D. student in the Department of Biosystems and Agricultural Engineering at Michigan State University, advised by Dr. Yuzhen Lu. My research focuses on developing advanced optical sensing systems and computer vision algorithms to address challenges in smart agriculture.

Prior to joining MSU, I earned my M.S. degree from Beijing Forestry University (BFU), where I worked on environment perception in complex forest scenes and the automation of forestry equipment. I completed my B.S. in Information and Computing Science at China University of Mining and Technology (Beijing).
