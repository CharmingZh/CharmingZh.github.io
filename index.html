About Me

I am currently a third-year master's student at Beijing Forestry University. My mentor is Prof. Kan Jiangming from BFU-Key Laboratory of State Forestry Administration on Forestry Equipment and Automation Labs. Prior to that, I was an undergraduate student in the College of Science at China University of Mining and Technology (Beijing), majoring in Information and Computing Science and minoring in Computer Science.

My main focus lies in Computer Vision, especially in environment perception in complex forest spaces. I try to optimize the pipeline of 3D representations of forests generated based on images and extract structural information of trees in that representation for automated forest inventory. My research work also involves the development of automated forestry and agricultural equipment using RGB or RGB-D as sensors.
